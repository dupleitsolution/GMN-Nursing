Nutrition Lab

Nutrition labs play a crucial role in educating future nurses about the significance of proper nutrition in patient care. These labs typically focus on various aspects such as understanding dietary requirements, assessing nutritional needs, and planning appropriate interventions.

Students learn about the role of different nutrients in promoting health and managing diseases. Hands-on activities may include meal planning, dietary assessments, and understanding special dietary needs for different patient populations.

Additionally, students may learn how to communicate effectively with patients about nutrition and lifestyle choices to promote overall well-being. These labs are essential in preparing nurses to address the nutritional aspects of patient care comprehensively.

OBJECTIVES
  • Understanding Macronutrients
    To analyze and comprehend the role of macronutrients (carbohydrates, proteins, and fats) in the human diet and their effects on health.
  • Assessing Micronutrient Intakes
    Assessing Micronutrient Intake: To evaluate the importance of vitamins and minerals in maintaining overall health and preventing nutrient deficiencies.
  • Dietary Analysis
    To learn methods for assessing dietary intake, such as food diaries, dietary recalls, and food frequency questionnaires, and understand their applications in nutrition research and practice.
  • Food Composition Analysis
    To examine the nutritional content of various foods through methods like proximate analysis, determining moisture content, ash content, protein content, fat content, carbohydrate content, and dietary fiber content.
  • Nutrition Label Interpretation
    To interpret and analyze nutrition labels on food products, understanding serving sizes, nutrient content, and the significance of different nutrients listed.
  • Nutrition Education
    To develop effective communication skills in conveying nutrition information to diverse populations, including strategies for promoting healthy eating habits and preventing diet-related diseases.
FACILITIES
  • Nutrition lab is equipped and well furnished with utensils and nutrition charts. It is well ventilated, lighting and adequate water facilities.
  • The students are imparted knowledge to assess the nutrition needs, caloric requirement of different age groups and during various disease conditions
