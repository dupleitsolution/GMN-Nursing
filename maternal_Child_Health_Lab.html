Maternal & Child Health Lab

Maternal and Child Health Nursing Lab is a pivotal specialty dedicated to supporting women throughout pregnancy, childbirth, and the postpartum period. The health and survival of both mother and child are integral to the wellbeing of society. A healthy mother is more likely to give birth to a healthy child. Therefore, providing comprehensive prenatal guidance and efficient care is vital for reducing maternal mortality and morbidity rates.

Maternal and Child Health Nursing (MCHN) laboratories serve as simulated environments where nursing students can apply theoretical knowledge into practical skills, enhancing their clinical competency and confidence in providing care for mothers and children.

OBJECTIVES
  • To provide basic and advanced skill of obstetric & gynaecological nursing
  • To reinforce learning using simulation technology and related resources.
  • To increase preparedness of the nursing students before introduction to hospital training.
  • To provide students with the nursing skills that are necessary to care for mother and child during physiological or pathological events.
  • To prepare students for handling well and sick children in a technically safe environment.
  • To provide opportunity for learning and practicing various child health nursing procedures.
  • To make the students informed regarding modern child care practices and guidelines.
